Hello, my name is Islena Ceballos

I am of Colombian origin. I hold a diploma in accounting and a diploma as a programmer-analyst in computer science. I have always had a deep passion for children, sports, and animals. A speed skater for ten years in my youth, I naturally developed values of perseverance and self-overcoming that I now pass on to the children in my care. Arriving in Canada in January 2011 as a permanent resident, I opened my family daycare service in 2012. This choice allowed me to reconcile my passion for early childhood education.

Why a home daycare?

I chose this model for its ability to offer children a stable, safe, and warm environment, with the same educator over several years. The multi-age group, like a family, fosters attachment, mutual aid, and respect. The older ones become role models for the younger ones, and everyone progresses at their own pace. The concrete advantages of our environment:

  • A respected ratio: 9 children maximum for 2 adults
  • A family, comfortable, and reassuring environment
  • Strong attachment through continuity with the same adults
  • Personalized support for each child
  • Open and regular communication with parents

Our mission

Contribute to the overall development of children by respecting the pace and particularities of each child. We offer a safe living environment where educational activities promote flourishing, equal opportunities, and socialization.

Our values

Respect, welcome, mutual aid, kindness, empathy, creativity, autonomy, perseverance, humor, and love. These values are transmitted daily through our presence, our listening, and our games. Each child is supported to understand the consequences of their actions and develop their self-confidence.

Our educational approach

Our program is structured around four stages: observation, planning, intervention, and reflection. Each child is observed daily to better understand their needs, interests, and strengths. Activities are planned accordingly, allowing ample room for the child's initiative. We prioritize:

  • Active play and free play as drivers of learning
  • Clear and reassuring routines that help the child orient themselves in time
  • Varied experiences: arts and crafts, music, science exploration, mathematics, reading, outings to the park
  • An individualized and caring approach
  • A democratic intervention style that encourages conflict resolution among peers
  • Special activities every Friday, shared with parents

Healthy lifestyle habits

Active play is at the heart of our daily routine. We regularly go to the park, play soccer, and enjoy the outdoors whenever the weather permits. Meals are served in a warm and respectful atmosphere. Naptime is accompanied by soft music and a story. Personal hygiene is encouraged through visual routines adapted to each child's age.

Our relationship with parents

Transparent and respectful communication is at the heart of our partnership with families. We share activities, menus, and photos via a private group on Facebook and Messenger, always prioritizing direct exchanges in the morning and at the end of the day.

In conclusion

Everything we do, our activities, our presence, our support aims to prepare each child for their school transition with confidence and joy. At the end of each year, we celebrate our "graduates" with a special party, souvenir photos, and a symbolic gift for their entry into school. Choosing a family environment governed by a coordinating office means choosing the safety, well-being, and flourishing of your child, in a setting that resembles them.
